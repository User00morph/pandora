### The Ephemeris Wheel

A 360° circle with planets placed at their current degree positions. The key reference points:

```
GEOCENTRIC WHEEL POINTS:
  Midheaven (X / MC): directly overhead for your trading location
                      For US stocks/ES: use New York, NY
                      Moves ~1° every 4 minutes
  Ascendant (A / ASC): Eastern horizon
                       Also moves ~1° every 4 minutes
  Descendant (DSC): Western horizon (180° from Ascendant)
  Imum Coeli (IC): directly below (180° from Midheaven)

These four points are the TRIGGERS that unlock aspect energy during the day.
The planets are the SETUP. The Ascendant/Midheaven are the TRIGGER.
```

### Aspects — The Core Measurement

```
ASPECT TABLE:
  Conjunction   0° / 360°    Division of circle by 1   — Most powerful
  Opposition    180°         Division by 2              — Very powerful
  Trine         120° / 240°  Division by 3              — Strong (triangles)
  Square        90° / 270°   Division by 4              — Strong (squares)
  Sextile       60° / 300°   Division by 6              — Moderate

APPLYING vs. SEPARATING:
  Applying: planets moving TOWARD the exact aspect angle

  Separating: planets moving AWAY from the exact angle
              — Energy is DISSIPATING. The release already happened.

EXACTNESS: The tighter the aspect (fewer degrees from exact), 
           the more powerful the signal. Under 1° = very high quality.
```

## CHAPTER 4: THE DECLINATION SYSTEM

This is Earik's primary long-term and swing trading tool. It creates a composite forecast wave that maps market highs and lows weeks and months in advance.

### The Formula

```
DECLINATION SYSTEM =
  (6 × Mercury Declination)
+ (5 × Venus Declination)
+ (4 × Earth Declination)
+ (3 × Mars Declination)
+ (2 × Jupiter Declination)
+ (1 × Saturn Declination)

Declination = the planet's angular distance NORTH or SOUTH 
              of the celestial equator (NOT the ecliptic position)

WEIGHTING RATIONALE:
  — Mercury gets the highest weight (6×) because it is closest to the Sun
    and therefore has the highest correlation to Solar Wind variations
  — Weights decrease with distance from the Sun
  — Earth's declination = the season effect (well-documented market seasonality)
  — Only inner planets used — outer planets (Uranus, Neptune, Pluto) move
    too slowly for swing trading purposes
```

### How to Read the Declination System

```
COMPOSITE WAVE BEHAVIOR:
  HIGH reading on the declination curve → market likely making a TOP
  LOW reading on the declination curve → market likely making a BOTTOM
  The curve acts as a drumbeat — markets dance to its rhythm

INVERSION RULE (critical):
  Sometimes markets INVERT relative to the forecast curve.
  When an inversion occurs, the market will remain inverted 
  until the NEXT turning point in the curve.
  Rule: if the market goes UP when the curve bottoms → it is inverted.
  Stay inverted until the curve turns again. Then re-assess.

FREQUENCY DOUBLING:
  In strong trending markets, the market will hit BOTH the curve tops 
  AND the halfpoints between them.
  The halfway point between two turning points = secondary signal.
  In a strong uptrend: lows appear at curve tops AND curve halfpoints.
  In a strong downtrend: highs appear at curve tops AND curve halfpoints.

QUARTER POINTS:
  Halfway between curve top and curve bottom = quarter point signal.
  These mark secondary reversals, consolidations, and continuation points.

HIDDEN CYCLE:
  A larger, longer-term cycle embedded within the visible declination curve.
  When the smaller cycles are all going in the same direction as the 
  hidden cycle → strong trends. When they diverge → choppy conditions.

LARGER CYCLE FRAMEWORK:
  The declination system nests inside a larger cycle (multi-year).
  Connect the declination TOPS with a line → longer-term trend.
  The small cycles oscillate around this larger trend direction.
```

### Application Example (from the book)

```
2002 Dow Jones case study:

The mechanism for timing intraday turns with planetary aspects. This is the session-level tool — used for entries within the day.

```

        — For New York: use New York latitude/longitude

STEP 7: At each watch time, look for price to form a high or low

EXAMPLE (July 18, 2007 — E-mini S&P):
  Setup: Moon opposite Uranus (very tight, high power)
         Mercury trine North Node (secondary setup)
  Trigger times:
    8:00am — ASC at Moon position
    9:40am — MC at Mercury position

  The forecast was correct to within 10 minutes.

```

  When three planets form a T-square (triangle on wheel):

    — Market often travels to that exact price and reverses
  Example: Moon at 0°, Jupiter at 90°, Node at 180° → empty corner at 270°
           270° maps to a specific price = the reversal target

KEY PRICE LEVELS:
  Any degree where multiple aspects converge = major price level
  Especially: aspects involving Saturn (structural) or Jupiter (expansive)

## CHAPTER 8: HOW TO ENTER

```
TWO ENTRY METHODS:

1. REVERSAL ENTRY (primary):
   Used when astrology signals a high-probability turning point.
   — Place LIMIT ORDER at the projected TIME and PRICE
   — Do not chase — if the price is missed, the trade is skipped
   — Stop: 2 points below the entry (for longs) or above (for shorts)
   — Target: 4 points profit = 2:1 risk/reward
   — If market confirms (bounces from level): hold to target
   — If market ignores the level: stop out, no argument

2. BREAKOUT ENTRY (secondary):
   Used when less certain about the exact reversal point.
   — Wait for the market to form a TOP or BOTTOM first
   — Enter when price BREAKS to new territory:
     Long: 1 tick above the recent consolidation high
     Short: 1 tick below the recent consolidation low
   — Misses some moves but avoids false starts
   — Better for confirmed trend resumptions

3. PULLBACK ENTRY (conservative):
   — Wait for reversal AND breakout to be confirmed
   — Enter on the first pullback after the breakout
   — Two lower lows from the high before entering long
   — Most patient approach — best for beginners

TRADE MANAGEMENT RULES (only two allowed):
  1. Move stop to breakeven once trade moves in your favor
  2. Cancel the order if a new opposing signal fires before entry fills

ONCE IN THE TRADE:
  — No more decisions. The rules are already set.
  — Exit at stop or target. Nothing else.
  — "You're either hit with a loss or out with a win."
```

## CHAPTER 9: HOW TO EXIT

```
THE EXIT IS MORE IMPORTANT THAN THE ENTRY.
"Your goal is not to pick every top and bottom. 
Your goal is to recreate the coin-flipping game."

PROFIT TARGET METHOD:
  Set target at 2× the stop distance.
  If stop is 2 points → target is 4 points.
  This locks in the 2:1 ratio mathematically.

CHART-BASED STOP:
  Stop = 1 tick beyond the most significant recent swing
  Target = entry + (distance to stop × 2)
  This ties your risk to the market's own structure, not an arbitrary number.

ACCURACY vs. EXPECTANCY — THE MASTER FORMULA:
  Accuracy = Avg Loss / (Avg Win + Avg Loss)
  
  To BREAK EVEN: Accuracy = 1/(1 + R:R)
    At 2:1 R:R: need 33% accuracy to break even
    At 3:1 R:R: need 25% accuracy to break even
    At 1:1 R:R: need 50% accuracy to break even
  
  Expected Profit Per Trade = (Accuracy × Avg Win) – ((1 – Accuracy) × Avg Loss)
  
  July 2007 live test results (E-mini S&P, buying the open):
    18 trades | 8 winners (44%) | Net: +7.25 pts/contract/month
  Same system selling the open:
    18 trades | 7 winners (38%) | Net: +6.75 pts/contract/month
  
  THE SECRET: wins were ~2.2× the average loss. That's the whole game.

PSYCHOLOGICAL RULE:
  Stop losses prevent you from "turning winners into losers."
  Moving a stop to breakeven is the one defensive action allowed.
  NEVER move a stop against the trade — that is account suicide.
