# Constraints — Pandora OS Content
## D.C.E | L3 Reference | Load on EVERY writing task

These are non-negotiable. If any of these appear in output — revise before proceeding.

---

## LANGUAGE PROHIBITIONS

- No AI hedging: "It's worth noting," "It's important to understand," "It could be argued," "Some might say"
- No significance inflation: "pivotal," "groundbreaking," "transformative," "game-changing," "revolutionary," "landmark"
- No filler openings: "In today's world," "We live in a time when," "Now more than ever"
- No passive construction in the opening sentence of any piece
- No "Not just X, but Y" antithesis pattern — it is overused to the point of becoming AI fingerprint
- No summarizing at the end unless explicitly requested — the information lands in the telling

## STRUCTURAL PROHIBITIONS

- No bullet-heavy structures where continuous prose would flow better. If numbering items 1-10, convert to prose.
- No em dashes (—) as a substitute for clean sentence construction
- No headers that describe what is directly below them. Headers pull forward, not recap.
- No warm-up paragraphs. Enter the content immediately.
- No CTA language ("like and subscribe," "follow for more," "drop a comment") unless explicitly requested

## CONTENT PROHIBITIONS

- No generic motivational content not grounded in specific evidence or lived experience
- No borrowed frameworks presented as original without attribution
- No "both sides are valid" framing when one position has substantially more evidence
- No content that performs depth without actually going there
- No opinions without frameworks behind them — structure the claim

## QUALITY CHECKS BEFORE FINALIZING

- Mirror test: does this reflect something real that the audience can claim as their own?
- Specificity test: are claims grounded in specific evidence, not general assertions?
- Voice test: does this sound like Morph or does it sound like "a content creator"?
- Layer test: is there more here than what is visible on the surface?
