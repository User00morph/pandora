# Format Patterns — Morph
## D.C.E | L3 Reference | Load when format is specified

One paragraph per format. What makes it structurally distinct.

---

## YOUTUBE — DECODED SERIES (research-backed, 15-30 min)
Thread a historical or ancient-to-modern story through the concept. The story carries the explanation. Start with a question the audience already has but has not been able to articulate. Midpoint: the reveal that reframes the entire opening. End: a principle the viewer can apply or investigate further. No summary recapping what was just said — the information lands in the telling, not the recap.

## YOUTUBE — PANDORA OS SERIES (tutorial/walkthrough, 8-15 min)
Open with the specific problem being solved. Show the actual filesystem, tool, or process — not slides about it. Narrate thinking as it happens. Tangents are allowed if they connect back. End with the working result and one clear takeaway about what this makes possible.

## YOUTUBE — SOVEREIGN MARKETS SERIES (analysis, 10-20 min)
Start with what the market is doing. Then: what the dominant narrative says it means. Then: what a different framework reveals. Walk through actual charts or patterns. Close with the decision the viewer now has information to make.

## YOUTUBE — EMPIRE LOGS SERIES (process doc, 5-10 min)
What happened this week. What was built, decided, or learned. What it cost (time, money, clarity). What changed in the thinking. Honest — not performance of honesty. The value is in the unfiltered reality, not the highlight reel.

## SHORT-FORM (TikTok / Reels, 60-90 seconds)
One insight only. No warm-up. Hook is the first sentence — it states the claim or asks the question. The middle proves or explores it in the shortest possible path. End with a reframe or a question, not a CTA. Under-explain rather than over-explain. The audience should want to know more.

## THREADS / X POSTS
Open tweet is the full argument in one sentence. Thread expands through evidence, examples, or steps. No filler tweets. Every tweet earns its place or gets cut. Close on an actionable or provocative idea — not "thoughts?" or "follow for more."

## LONG-FORM WRITTEN (Substack / articles)
More room for the full architecture of an idea. Can go deeper into sources and history than video. Same voice — the depth, not the style, is what changes. No headers that summarize the content directly below them. Headers should pull the reader forward, not summarize what they are about to read.

## PODCAST (audio-only, research-heavy content)
Slower pace than video. The listener cannot see anything. Concepts need to land in words alone. Shorter sentences when complexity rises. Recaps between major sections are appropriate here — unlike video where they feel redundant. Open with what the episode is actually about — no extended preamble.
