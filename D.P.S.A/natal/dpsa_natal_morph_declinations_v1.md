## DOCTRINE

**Declination** is a planet's angular distance north (+) or south (−) of the celestial equator. Where longitude measures position around the zodiac belt, declination measures distance above or below the equatorial plane. Two planets at the same declination are **parallel** — a hidden aspect equivalent to a conjunction in strength. Two planets at equal but opposite declinations are **contra-parallel** — equivalent to an opposition.

**Out-of-Bounds (OOB):** The Sun never exceeds ±23°26' declination (the ecliptic obliquity). When any other planet exceeds this limit, it is operating beyond the solar system's "authorized" range. OOB planets are considered liberated from solar authority — they express their archetype without the solar system's normative constraints. They can be extreme, uncontrollable, or genius-level depending on the planet and chart.

Contra-parallels and parallels are "hidden aspects" — they operate beneath the surface of the tropical chart and are invisible to standard aspect analysis. They reveal structural connections that exist in three-dimensional space even when no zodiacal aspect is present.
