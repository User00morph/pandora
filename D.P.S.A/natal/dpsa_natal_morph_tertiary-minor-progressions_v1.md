## DOCTRINE

Secondary progressions (1 day = 1 year) are the primary Western progression system — already documented in `dpsa_natal_morph_progressions-profection-2026_v1.md`. Two additional progression systems offer complementary time-scale lenses:

**Tertiary Progressions:** 1 calendar day = 1 lunar month (29.53 days)  
Rate: For age 20, advance the natal chart by 20 × 29.53 = 590.6 days from birth.

**Minor Progressions:** 1 lunar month = 1 year  
Rate: 1 lunar month (29.53 days) = 1 year. For age 20, advance by 20 × 29.53 = 590.6 days from birth.

Both systems produce the same equivalent date: birth + 590.6 days ≈ **December 24, 2007** (approximately 590 days after May 12, 2006).

The equivalence occurs because both systems use the same base ratio (1 lunar month per year) — just expressed differently. Tertiary progressions move faster than secondary but land on the same "progressed chart" when both are computed for the same age. The distinction between them becomes meaningful primarily when computing sub-period themes within a year rather than the year-age chart itself.
