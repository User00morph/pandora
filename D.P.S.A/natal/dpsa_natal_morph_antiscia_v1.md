## DOCTRINE

**Antiscia** (singular: antiscion) are mirror-point placements along the Cancer/Capricorn solstice axis. A planet at degree X has its antiscion at the degree of equal solar declination on the opposite side of the solstice — the day of equal day-length. Two planets in antiscion are in a covert, hidden structural alliance: they share the same solar declination, meaning the Sun illuminates both points with equal light at different times of the year.

**Formula:** Antiscion = (90° − [degree within sign]) reflected across the nearest solstice axis (0° Cancer / 0° Capricorn).

**Contrascia** (contra-antiscia) are the mirror across the Aries/Libra equinox axis — points of equal night length. Where antiscia are solar, contrascia are equinoctial.

**Interpretation principle:** Antiscia relationships reveal hidden structural alliances between planets that do not share a classical aspect. They function as covert connections — the planets "see" each other across the solstice mirror even when no direct line exists in the tropical chart.
