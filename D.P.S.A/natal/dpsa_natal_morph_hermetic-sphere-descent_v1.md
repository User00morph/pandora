## DOCTRINE: THE HERMETIC COSMOLOGICAL MODEL

### The Seven Planetary Spheres

In Hermetic, Neoplatonic, and pre-modern cosmological tradition, the soul does not arrive directly in the body from the highest divine realm. It descends through a series of **seven planetary spheres**, receiving from each sphere a specific quality — a capacity, a limitation, a gift, and a challenge — before entering the material world through the lunar gate (the final threshold before incarnation).

This doctrine appears in:
- **Corpus Hermeticum (Poimandres):** The soul descends through the seven zones, abandoning capacities to each sphere on the way down; the task of the life is to reclaim what was deposited through spiritual development.
- **Macrobius's Commentary (5th century CE):** The soul receives specific capacities at each sphere during descent: the rational faculty at Saturn, the practical capacity at Jupiter, the irascible capacity at Mars, the perceptive and imaginative at the Sun, the appetitive (desire) at Venus, the expressive capacity at Mercury, and the vegetative (growth) at the Moon.
- **Porphyry's Nymphs:** The Cave of the Nymphs describes the descent through Cancer (the gate of souls' descent into matter) and ascent through Capricorn (the gate of souls' return to the divine). Cancer ASC is particularly significant here.

### The Descent Sequence

**Saturn → Jupiter → Mars → Sun → Venus → Mercury → Moon → Earth**

The soul passes through the outermost sphere first (Saturn — the limit of the traditional cosmos) and arrives last through the Moon (the threshold of material incarnation). Each sphere is a gate through which the soul passes and from which it receives or deposits qualities.

### The Ascent Sequence (the life's work)

**Moon → Mercury → Venus → Sun → Mars → Jupiter → Saturn → Beyond**

The task of the incarnated life is the reverse: to reclaim and elevate through each planetary domain, moving from the lunar-material back toward the Saturnine boundary and ultimately beyond it. Each natal planet marks where the soul's work with that sphere's qualities stands — what was received, what was carried, and what must be refined.
