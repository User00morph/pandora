## DOCTRINE

The **Tzolkin** (Mayan: "count of days") is the 260-day sacred calendar of Mesoamerican civilization — used by the Maya, Aztec (as the Tonalpohualli), Zapotec, and numerous other Mesoamerican cultures for at least 3,000 years. It is the oldest continuously used calendar system still in operation.

The Tzolkin has no astronomical basis — it does not track the Sun, Moon, or planets. It is a sacred mathematical structure: **20 Day Signs × 13 Tones = 260 unique day-combinations**. The 260-day cycle was understood by the Maya as the fundamental pulse of creation — the span of human gestation, the sacred round of agricultural and ritual life, and the template of consciousness itself.

The **Day Sign** at birth is the soul's primary archetypal signature — the fundamental quality of the day-energy one was born into and carries through life.

The **Tone** is the numeric frequency (1-13) that qualifies the Day Sign — the "how" and "why" of the day's expression.

## THE TZOLKIN DAY: 4 CIMI

### CIMI — The Day Sign of Death and Transformation

**Cimi** (in Yucatec Maya; also known as **Miquiztli** in Aztec/Nahuatl) is the sixth day sign in the Tzolkin sequence. It is the day sign of death, passage, transformation, and the surrender to the greater cycle.

**Cimi's glyph:** Often depicted as a death's head or skull — not in the Western sense of mortality-as-fear but in the Mesoamerican sense of death as the fundamental engine of transformation and regeneration. The skull is the symbol of the ancestor, the transformed one, the being that has passed through the gate and emerged on the other side.

**Cimi's core qualities:**
- **Surrender to the larger cycle:** Cimi is the energy of releasing individual will to the larger intelligence that moves through death and rebirth. Not passive surrender but the active recognition that something must end for the next thing to begin.
- **The passage between worlds:** Cimi people are natural bridgers between states — between the visible and invisible, between what has been and what will be, between the lived form and the truth beneath form.
- **Transformation through release:** The Cimi function is not destroying for its own sake but releasing what has completed its cycle. Cimi knows when something is done.
- **Connection to ancestral intelligence:** Cimi carries a strong ancestral dimension — sensitivity to what has been passed down through lineage, both as gift and as what needs to be transformed.
- **Healer and death-walker:** In Mesoamerican tradition, Cimi people were often associated with the healing arts (specifically with the medicine that assists transition — including death midwifery), with divination (access to the invisible), and with the shamanic capacity to walk between states.

**Cimi and the Tzolkin's deeper structure:**
Cimi is the 6th sign — the midpoint of the first 13-sign sequence (the first "wave" of the Tzolkin). It is the sign that stands at the threshold between the first movement (establishing the new) and the second movement (transforming what was established). Cimi holds the hinge between creation and transformation.

### TONE 4 — The Self-Existing Tone (The Stability Tone)

**Tone 4** is the self-existing, stable, form-giving tone. Of the 13 tones:
- Tones 1-3: establish the impulse and energize it
- **Tone 4: gives the impulse stable form — definition, measurement, structure**
- Tones 5-7: radiate, organize, and attune the form
- Tones 8-13: manifest, complete, and transcend

**Tone 4 qualities:**
- **Self-existing:** The tone that doesn't need external validation to define itself — it measures from its own inner standard
- **Form-giving:** Tone 4 takes the creative impulse (established by tones 1-3) and gives it stable, measurable form — the blueprint, the structure, the definition
- **Stability:** Not rigid stability but the stability of a well-defined foundation — knowing what one is, standing in that knowing
- **Measurement:** Tone 4 is associated with the capacity to measure, define, and set boundaries — the function of discernment applied to giving things their proper form

**The Tone 4 developmental question:** *What is the stable form that defines my purpose? How do I give structure to what I know?*
