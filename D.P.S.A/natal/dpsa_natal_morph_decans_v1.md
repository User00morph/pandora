## 0. WHAT THE DECAN LAYER IS

The zodiac divides into 12 signs of 30° each. Each sign divides further into 3 decans of 10° each — 36 total. The decans predate the Greek zodiac. They are Kemetic: the original 36 decan stars governed the Egyptian 360-day calendar, each rising heliacally for a 10-day period to mark the passage of the year. Sirius (Sopdet) was the crown decan — its heliacal rising announced the Nile flood and the new year.

The Hellenistic tradition systematized the Kemetic decans into the **Chaldean Face sequence**: planets are assigned to decans in the Chaldean order (Saturn → Jupiter → Mars → Sun → Venus → Mercury → Moon → repeat), beginning with Mars ruling Aries Decan 1 (Aries being the sign Mars rules). This creates 36 distinct 10° chambers, each under a specific planetary intelligence.

The face ruler of a decan is a **dignity**. A planet in its own face has dignity by face — partial, but real. It functions with more internal coherence than it would in a decan it does not rule. Face dignity does not overcome detriment or fall — but it modifies them. A planet in detriment in its own face is less dysfunctional than the sign-level detriment alone suggests.

**Reading the decan layer surfaces what sign-only astrology cannot:**
- Hidden planetary governance that creates structural patterns invisible at the sign level
- Decan ruler mutual relationships between planets (governance chains)
- The specific 10° archetypal chamber a planet operates within — narrower and more precise than the sign alone
- Direct connection to the original Kemetic daemon-spirits who governed each 10° interval
