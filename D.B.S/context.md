**The body has intelligence:**
The human body maintains homeostasis across thousands of simultaneous variables without conscious direction. It repairs tissue, fights infection, regulates temperature, digests food, and generates entirely new cells — all without being told to. This is not mechanical function. It is intelligence operating at a level of complexity that no medical system fully understands. Working with that intelligence rather than overriding it is the foundation of sovereign health.

These traditions were not primitive — they represented accumulated empirical knowledge developed over thousands of years. They were actively suppressed, not superseded.

**The inversion:**
The dominant system inverts the relationship between the person and their health. In the sovereign model, the person understands their body, knows what serves it, and uses practitioners as consultants when needed. In the inverted model, the person is dependent on external authority for permission, diagnosis, and prescribed intervention — and is systematically kept ignorant of their own biological intelligence. D.B.S works to invert this inversion.

**Honest evidence standards:**
Holistic and traditional health claims exist on a wide spectrum of evidence quality — from rigorous ethnobotanical research and clinical trials on specific herbs, to tradition-based knowledge with no formal study, to pure commercial claims. D.B.S applies the D.R.D source tier standard. What is documented is documented. What is traditional is honored as tradition. What is speculative is flagged as speculative. The goal is genuine knowledge, not confirmation of a preferred narrative.

This library documents medicinal plants with honest attention to both traditional use and available evidence. Each entry covers:

```
EVIDENCE TIER: Rated by D.R.D standard
```

### Initial Herb Entries

**Sea Moss (Chondrus crispus / Genus Gracilaria)**
Tradition: Caribbean, West African, Irish coastal traditions
Documented properties: Rich in iodine, potassium, magnesium, and other minerals. Carrageenan content has documented mucilaginous properties relevant to digestive support.
Traditional uses: Respiratory support, thyroid function (via iodine), digestive lining, energy
Preparation: Gel form (soaked and blended), capsule, smoothie addition
Contraindications: High iodine content — not appropriate in excess for those with thyroid conditions. Quality varies significantly — wildcrafted preferred over pool-grown.
Evidence tier: Tier 2-3 — traditional use well-documented, specific health claims at varying evidence levels

**Spirulina (Arthrospira platensis)**
Documented properties: High-protein microalgae with documented iron, B12 (though bioavailability debated), and antioxidant content. Anti-inflammatory properties have clinical study support.
Traditional uses: Food source — used by Aztec civilization and others
Preparation: Powder in water or smoothie, capsule
Contraindications: Source quality matters — heavy metal contamination possible in low-quality products
Evidence tier: Tier 1-2 — better studied than most supplements

**Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)**
Tradition: Ayurvedic medicine — one of the most documented adaptogens
Documented properties: Cortisol reduction, stress response modulation — multiple randomized controlled trials support this. Thyroid hormone effects also documented.
Traditional uses: Energy, stress adaptation, cognitive function, male reproductive health
Preparation: Root powder, capsule, ashwagandha ghee in traditional preparation
Contraindications: Autoimmune conditions — caution. Thyroid medication — interaction possible.
Evidence tier: Tier 1 — among the best-studied herbs in clinical literature

**Chaga (Inonotus obliquus)**
Tradition: Siberian and Northern European folk medicine
Documented properties: High antioxidant content — ORAC values documented. Beta-glucan content with immunomodulatory properties studied.
Traditional uses: Immune support, digestive health, anti-inflammatory
Preparation: Decoction (long simmer, not tea steep), tincture, dual extract for both water and alcohol soluble compounds
Contraindications: Anticoagulant medications — interaction. Oxalate content — relevant for kidney stone history.
Evidence tier: Tier 2-3 — traditional use extensive, some cell and animal study, limited human clinical trials

**Lion's Mane (Hericium erinaceus)**
Documented properties: Nerve Growth Factor (NGF) stimulation — documented in in vitro studies. Small human studies on cognitive function and mood show promising results.
Traditional uses: Traditional Chinese and Japanese medicine — cognitive support
Preparation: Dual extract for both beta-glucans and hericenones/erinacines. Cooking or extraction required — raw is less bioavailable.
Contraindications: Mushroom allergy. Drug interactions not fully characterized.
Evidence tier: Tier 2 — early human clinical data promising, larger trials needed

**Moringa (Moringa oleifera)**
Tradition: Used across Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, and the Caribbean
Documented properties: Exceptional nutritional density — documented across multiple analyses. Contains all essential amino acids, high vitamin and mineral content.
Traditional uses: Nutrition supplementation, anti-inflammatory, milk production support for nursing mothers
Preparation: Leaf powder, fresh leaves, seed oil
Contraindications: Pregnancy — uterine contraction stimulation documented in some forms. Medication interactions not fully studied.
Evidence tier: Tier 1-2 for nutritional content; Tier 2-3 for medicinal claims

## THE NERVOUS SYSTEM AS FOUNDATION

The nervous system is the primary mediator between internal state and physical health. This is not a metaphor — it is documented physiology.

**The autonomic nervous system:**
```
SYMPATHETIC — "fight or flight"
Activates for threat response: elevated cortisol and adrenaline,
increased heart rate, blood flow to muscles,
digestion suspended, immune function suppressed.
Designed for acute threat — not chronic activation.

PARASYMPATHETIC — "rest and digest"
Restoration, digestion, immune function, cellular repair,
reproductive function, learning consolidation.
This is where healing happens.
Chronic sympathetic dominance prevents healing.
```

**Heart Rate Variability (HRV):**
The variation in time between heartbeats. High HRV indicates a nervous system that can flexibly respond to changing demands — moving between sympathetic and parasympathetic efficiently. Low HRV indicates a system locked in chronic stress activation. HRV is one of the most useful measurable indicators of overall physiological resilience.

**The vagus nerve:**
The primary nerve of the parasympathetic system, running from the brainstem through the body. Vagal tone — the strength and responsiveness of vagal function — is a significant determinant of health across multiple systems including cardiovascular, digestive, and immune. Vagal tone can be trained.

**Practical protocols for nervous system support:**
- Slow, extended exhale breathing (4-count in, 6-8 count out) — directly stimulates vagal response
- Cold exposure — cold water on the face or cold shower — vagal stimulation
- Humming, singing, chanting — vibrates the vagus nerve through the throat
- HRV biofeedback — using a heart rate monitor to train coherent breathing
- Sleep quality — the foundation that everything else rests on
