# SKILL — IEC Phase Detection
**Institutional Expansion Cycle | 5-Phase Identification Protocol**
**Load when:** Reading any instrument's current position in the institutional cycle.

---

## WHAT THIS IS

Price does not move randomly. It moves in a repeating 5-phase institutional cycle: institutions accumulate (buy cheap), trap retail (induce early entries in the wrong direction), expand (deliver to target), exhaust (run final retail FOMO), and mitigate (return to fix unfilled orders). Identifying which phase an instrument is in tells you: is this a setup, a trap, or a wait?

**Source:** Nathan Banks (@tradewithbanks) — IEC (Institutional Expansion Cycle)

---

## THE 5 PHASES

### PHASE 1 — ACCUMULATION
**What institutions are doing:** Buying below market value quietly. No visible intent.
**What price looks like:**
- Range-bound, choppy, seemingly directionless
- Small-body candles, frequent wicks in both directions
- Multiple tests of the same support/resistance zone without breaking
- Volume: average or below average
- EMAs: compressing, converging, tangled

**What retail does:** Gets frustrated, abandons the pair, or enters random directions
**What this tells you:** Do not trade. Observe. The range IS the accumulation zone.
**Alchemical phase:** Nigredo — the dissolution before form emerges

---

### PHASE 2 — IMPULSE TRAP (Wyckoff Spring / Distribution Test)
**What institutions are doing:** Creating a false move to collect stop losses and attract retail entries in the wrong direction before the real move.
**What price looks like:**
- Strong, convincing move OPPOSITE to the actual institutional direction
- Breaks a significant level (PDH, equal highs, support) — looks like a breakout
- Retail enters: "The breakout is confirmed!"
- Quick reversal — the move is not sustained
- The candle that breaks the level often has a long wick back through it
- Volume: high on the fake move (institutions absorbing retail orders)

**What retail does:** Buys the breakout (gets trapped long) or sells the breakdown (gets trapped short)
**What this tells you:** This is the setup formation. The sweep of liquidity IS the manipulation criterion for Grade A.
**Alchemical phase:** Albedo — the false light before the real illumination

**Key question:** Where did stops accumulate above/below? That is where the trap will run.

---

### PHASE 3 — EXPANSION (Delivery)
**What institutions are doing:** Delivering price to target. The real directional move.
**What price looks like:**
- Strong, clean directional movement
- Large candle bodies, small wicks relative to body size
- Little retracement — price "stair-steps" in the direction
- Breaks structure consistently in the delivery direction
- EMAs separating, spreading in the direction of the move
- Volume: elevated on expansion candles

**What retail does:** Chases price (too late), or waits for a pullback that never comes (deep enough to enter safely)
**What this tells you:** This is the trade delivery zone — enter early expansion or on a shallow retracement to the 50% of the last expansion candle.
**Alchemical phase:** Citrinitas — the yellowing, gold emerging, the real thing beginning to show

**Two-Hour Clock:** Once expansion begins, the institutional move typically completes within 2 hours. Start the clock.

---

### PHASE 4 — EXHAUSTION
**What institutions are doing:** Beginning to exit positions. Using retail FOMO buying to sell into.
**What price looks like:**
- Momentum slowing — candles getting smaller in the direction of the trend
- Wicks beginning to appear against the trend direction
- "Blow-off top" or "blow-off bottom" — one final push that looks like the trend is accelerating, then immediate reversal
- Volume: high but price not moving proportionally (distribution)
- EMAs: price extended far above/below — "rubber band" stretched

**What retail does:** "It's going forever!" — enters late (provides exit liquidity for institutions)
**What this tells you:** Do NOT enter in the trend direction. Begin watching for Phase 5 mitigation setup.
**Alchemical phase:** Late Citrinitas → approaching Rubedo

---

### PHASE 5 — MITIGATION (Return)
**What institutions are doing:** Returning to the origin zone of the expansion to fill remaining orders.
**What price looks like:**
- Price returns to the 50% level of the Phase 3 expansion candle (the mitigation block)
- Or: returns to the original accumulation zone
- The return often looks slow, grinding, "corrective"
- May form another accumulation zone at the mitigation level before the next expansion

**What retail does:** "The trend is reversing!" — shorts the mitigation (gets trapped again)
**What this tells you:** This is the highest-probability re-entry point for the original direction. The 50% mitigation is the institutional re-fill zone.
**Alchemical phase:** Rubedo — completion, then the cycle begins again

## DETECTION PROTOCOL

**Step 1 — Set the timeframe**
Use the HTF to identify the macro IEC phase (Daily or 4H). Use the ITF for entry timing (1H or 15M).

**Step 2 — Read the last significant move**
Where did price come FROM? Where did it GO? How long did it take?

**Step 3 — Apply the 5-phase sequence**
Read the candle structure from left to right. Label each phase:
- Range with no direction = Accumulation
- Strong move + reversal + retail trapped = Impulse Trap
- Clean directional delivery = Expansion
- Momentum fading, late retail FOMO = Exhaustion
- Return to 50% of expansion origin = Mitigation

**Step 4 — Identify current position**
Which phase is ACTIVE right now? Where in that phase?

**Step 5 — Trade implication**
```
Phase 1 (Accumulation)    → Wait. No trade. Map the range boundaries.
Phase 2 (Impulse Trap)    → Watch for sweep completion. Prepare Grade A filter.
Phase 3 (Expansion)       → Trade direction = post-sweep direction. Entry: early expansion or 50% retracement.
Phase 4 (Exhaustion)      → No new entries in trend direction. Begin watching for cycle reset.
Phase 5 (Mitigation)      → Watch for Grade A re-entry at the 50% mitigation level.
```

## THE "NOT IN CYCLE" FLAG

> "If the IEC cycle phase cannot be clearly identified on an instrument — skip it. Not all pairs are tradeable at all times."

**When to flag:** If after completing the detection protocol you cannot clearly identify which phase price is in, or if two phases seem equally plausible → this instrument is NOT in a readable cycle state. Log it as "NIC — Not In Cycle" and move to the next pair.

**A "not in cycle" read is valuable data.** It means the institutional agenda is unclear on this pair right now. Forcing a read is the same as justifying an entry — a program signal.

## COMMON MISTAKES

| Mistake | What Happened | What To Do Instead |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Entering in Phase 1 (range) | Accumulation looks like "stable support" | Wait for Phase 2 trap to form |
| Entering on Phase 2 fake breakout | Impulse trap looks like real expansion | Wait for the reversal BACK through the level |
| Missing Phase 3 entry (waiting for "safe" pullback) | Price expands without deep retracement | Enter on the first 50% candle retracement in Phase 3 |
| Entering Phase 4 late (FOMO) | Momentum looks strongest right before exhaustion | Phase 4 = exits only, never entries |
| Fading Phase 5 mitigation (shorting the "correction") | Mitigation looks like reversal | Mitigation = re-entry in original direction, not counter-trade |
