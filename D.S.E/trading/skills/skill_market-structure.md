## WHAT THIS IS

Market structure is the skeleton of price. Before any session analysis, IEC phase detection, or entry evaluation — the structural bias must be established on the higher timeframe. A correct structural read tells you: which direction are institutions CURRENTLY delivering price, and where will they deliver it TO?

**Source:** Kim — IC4XCLEARLY. Todd — 6-Month candle framing cascade.

## THE TOP-DOWN CASCADE (how to read structure)

Structure must be established in this exact order — from highest to lowest timeframe. A setup without HTF confirmation is not a setup.

```
STEP 1 — 6-MONTH CANDLE (Todd)
Close direction = institutional bias for the current 6-month period.
The 6M candle close is the master bias. All lower timeframes must align.
Power of 3 at 6M scale: A (accumulation, months 1-2) → M (manipulation, fake break) → D (distribution, real move)

STEP 2 — MONTHLY
Is monthly structure aligned with 6M bias?
Look for: recent BOS on monthly → confirms 6M direction.
If monthly is ranging or CHoCH signal → flag for review.

STEP 3 — WEEKLY
Is weekly structure confirming monthly?
Mark weekly highs/lows. Has there been a clean BOS in the 6M direction?
Monday Gold Box: the week's first 8 hours set the week's high/low range.

STEP 4 — DAILY
Where is the specific key level / entry zone for this week?
Identify: PDH/PDL, daily order blocks, daily equal highs/lows.
The daily key level is where price will be engineered to reach for the intraday entry.

STEP 5 — INTRADAY (1H / 15M)
Wait for price to reach the daily key level, then evaluate the entry setup.
Only at the daily key level. Not before. Not mid-range.
```

**Rule:** "No intraday entry without HTF alignment. Strict top-down cascade." — Todd

## BOS vs. CHoCH — WHAT THEY MEAN

### BOS — Break of Structure (confirmed direction change)
**Definition:** Price breaks a significant swing high (in an uptrend) or swing low (in a downtrend) and holds above/below it.
**What it confirms:** The structural direction has shifted. A new trend leg is beginning in the BOS direction.
**Only valid on:** Significant swing highs/lows — not every candle high/low.
**How to identify:**
- In an uptrend: price takes out the last SIGNIFICANT high AND holds above it on close
- In a downtrend: price takes out the last SIGNIFICANT low AND holds below it on close
**Trade implication:** When BOS confirms on a higher timeframe → bias shifts to BOS direction for all lower TF entries.

### CHoCH — Change of Character (early reversal warning)
**Definition:** A lower timeframe structural shift that signals a possible reversal BEFORE HTF BOS confirms.
**What it signals:** Caution. The momentum is changing but the HTF bias has not flipped yet.
**How to identify:**
- In an uptrend: price fails to make a new high AND then breaks a recent swing low
- In a downtrend: price fails to make a new low AND then breaks a recent swing high
**Trade implication:** CHoCH alone = no trade in opposite direction. Wait for BOS confirmation on the relevant timeframe. CHoCH = watch. BOS = act.

### The sequence:
```
CHoCH on 15M → possible reversal signal → watch
CHoCH on 1H → stronger signal → prepare
BOS on 1H + CHoCH on 4H → structural shift confirmed on ITF → entry valid
BOS on 4H → HTF structure flipped → highest confidence
```

## ORDER BLOCKS — WHERE INSTITUTIONAL ORDERS WERE PLACED

**Definition:** The last opposing candle body before a significant directional move.

**Bullish Order Block:**
- A bearish candle (red) that is the LAST bearish candle before a strong bullish impulse
- Institutions placed BUY orders here — when price returns to this zone, those orders are still waiting
- Entry zone: the candle's high-to-low range, with focus on the 50% level

**Bearish Order Block:**
- A bullish candle (green) that is the LAST bullish candle before a strong bearish impulse
- Institutions placed SELL orders here — when price returns, those orders fill
- Entry zone: the candle's high-to-low range

**How to identify:**
1. Find a strong impulsive move (3+ large consecutive candles, same direction)
2. Look at the LAST candle before that impulse that was in the OPPOSITE direction
3. That candle's body = the order block zone
4. When price returns to that zone → potential mitigation entry

**Validity:**
- Order block is VALID until price closes through it (mitigated)
- Price touching the order block = institutional re-fill in progress
- Entering on the return to an order block = IEC Phase 5 (Mitigation) entry

## SUPPLY AND DEMAND ZONES

**Supply zone:** Where institutions previously SOLD aggressively — where unfilled sell orders remain
**Demand zone:** Where institutions previously BOUGHT aggressively — where unfilled buy orders remain

**Difference from order blocks:** Zones are larger ranges (often created by consolidation before a move). Order blocks are single-candle precision.

**How to identify supply zones:**
1. Find a significant bearish impulse
2. The consolidation or range BEFORE the impulse = supply zone
3. Price returning to this zone = institutional sell orders being re-filled

**How to identify demand zones:**
1. Find a significant bullish impulse
2. The consolidation or range BEFORE the impulse = demand zone
3. Price returning to this zone = institutional buy orders being re-filled

**Zone-to-Zone trading (Kim's core principle):**
Price moves from demand zone to supply zone (bullish) or supply zone to demand zone (bearish). Enter at one zone, target the next. NEVER enter mid-range.

```
Bullish: Enter at DEMAND zone → Target nearest SUPPLY zone
Bearish: Enter at SUPPLY zone → Target nearest DEMAND zone
```

## FIBONACCI — ENTRY PRECISION WITHIN ZONES

**Use only as precision tool inside an already-identified structural zone.** Not as a standalone signal.

**Key levels:**
- 0.618 — primary golden ratio retracement entry
- 0.705 — secondary precision level
- 0.79 — deep retracement entry (last valid level before invalidation)

**Application:**
1. Identify the structural swing (BOS to BOS, or key high to key low)
2. Draw Fibonacci from swing high to swing low (or vice versa)
3. Wait for price to retrace INTO the 0.618-0.79 zone
4. Enter when price shows rejection from the zone (confirmation candle)

**Discount vs. Premium:**
- For LONG entries: enter in the "discount" zone (below 0.5 = below midpoint of the swing)
- For SHORT entries: enter in the "premium" zone (above 0.5 = above midpoint of the swing)

## COMMON STRUCTURE MISTAKES

| Mistake | What Happened | Correction |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| Trading a CHoCH as a BOS | Entered reversal without confirmation | Wait for BOS on the same or higher TF |
| Entering mid-range "because structure looks bullish" | No key level present | Zone-to-zone only — enter at the zone |
| Using every candle high/low as structure | Too many "levels" — noise not signal | Only SIGNIFICANT swings (multi-candle highs/lows) |
| Counter-trading HTF BOS with ITF entry | ITF signal against the institutional tide | Top-down cascade must align — HTF > ITF |
| Calling a "mitigated" order block a valid entry | Price already closed through it | Mitigated = gone. Find the next unmitigated block |
