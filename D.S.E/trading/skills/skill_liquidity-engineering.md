## THE CORE INVERSION

The single most important reframe in this framework:

> **Equal highs and equal lows are NOT resistance and support. They are advertisements.**

When price creates two highs at the same level, every retail technical trader sees "resistance." They place their sell stops just above it. Those stops — when triggered — become BUY orders (to close their shorts) that institutions need to fill their large SELL positions. The level is not blocking price. It is ATTRACTING price — because the stops sitting there are the liquidity.

**Equal highs → stops above → institutional sell entry point**
**Equal lows → stops below → institutional buy entry point**

This is the mechanism behind the Impulse Trap (IEC Phase 2) and the Asia Grab. Now apply it everywhere.

## FIVE LIQUIDITY POOLS (where stops cluster)

### 1. Equal Highs / Equal Lows
**Definition:** Two or more candle highs/lows at the same price level.
**Why it's a target:** Maximum retail stop clustering. The more "obvious" the level, the more retail positions it with stops.
**How institutions use it:** Price is engineered to sweep above/below equal highs/lows — collecting stops as fuel — then reverses.
**How to identify:** Visual double top/bottom where the two wicks are within 3-5 pips.
**Priority:** HIGHEST — these are the primary daily targets.

---

### 2. Round Numbers (xx.000, xx.500, xx.100)
**Definition:** Psychologically significant price levels ending in .000, .500, .100, .050.
**Why it's a target:** Retail places stops at round numbers by default. Maximum stop density.
**How institutions use it:** Round numbers are swept on the way to an actual institutional target. Or they ARE the target.
**Key rule:** When a round number coincides with equal highs/lows → highest probability sweep target.
**Example:** EURUSD 1.1000 with equal highs at 1.0998 = maximum liquidity concentration.

---

### 3. Previous Day High / Previous Day Low (PDH / PDL)
**Definition:** The high and low of the previous trading day's session.
**Why it's a target:** Breakout traders place entries just above PDH (buy stops) and just below PDL (sell stops). These sit there waiting.
**How institutions use it:** Price regularly sweeps PDH or PDL at session open — collecting these stops — before reversing. This is the London sweep of Asian range applied at the daily level.
**Daily habit:** Mark PDH and PDL every morning before session open.

---

### 4. Session Highs / Session Lows
**Definition:** The high and low of the current or most recent major session (Asia / London / NY).
**Why it's a target:** Session extremes attract stop clusters just as PDH/PDL do, but at the intraday level.
**Key application:** London sweep of Asia high/low = the daily direction signal (see skill_session-architecture.md). NY sweep of London high/low = secondary direction confirmation.
**Priority during London open:** Mark Asia High and Asia Low as the immediate sweep targets.

---

### 5. Weekly and Monthly Highs / Lows
**Definition:** The structural high and low of the current or previous week/month.
**Why it's a target:** Institutional-scale stop clusters. Higher timeframe traders accumulate stops here.
**How institutions use it:** The Monday Gold Box identifies the week's initial range. Tuesday typically sweeps one of Monday's extremes to establish direction. Weekly high/low sweeps are Three-Hit Reversal candidates.
**Priority for swing positions:** Always mark the current week's high and low before evaluating setups.

## NEWS AS COVER — THE INSTITUTIONAL COVER STORY

This is one of the most misunderstood dynamics in retail trading:

> **Institutions do NOT react to news. They position BEFORE news. News provides the retail story for a position already built.**

**The sequence (what actually happens):**
1. Institution identifies the upcoming event (Fed announcement, CPI, NFP)
2. Institution builds position in the days/hours BEFORE the announcement — during the Accumulation phase
3. The announcement releases — creates a spike (the "news move")
4. Retail reacts to the news and enters in the direction of the spike
5. The spike IS the Impulse Trap — institution uses retail buying/selling to exit their position
6. Price reverses — retail is stuck

**Signs the news event is a cover story:**
- Strong directional move into the announcement (Impulse Trap building)
- COT retail % already extreme in the news direction before release
- Price sweeps a significant equal high/low during or immediately after announcement
- The move reverses within 1-2 hours of the announcement (Expansion fires opposite)

**Rule:** Never trade the announcement. Trade the AFTERMATH — the rejection from the sweep level.

## COT THRESHOLDS — CROWD POSITIONING AS LIQUIDITY MAP

COT (Commitments of Traders) data quantifies where retail stops are concentrated:

| COT Level | Meaning | Institutional Action |
|-----------|---------|---------------------|
| >65-70% retail long | Maximum crowd belief | Sell target — institutions need retail long stops as SELL liquidity |
| 55-65% retail long | Crowd building | Watch for sweep developing — not at extreme yet |
| ~50% retail | Neutral | No strong crowd signal — look to other layers |
| <35% retail long | Retail crowded SHORT | Buy target — institutions need retail short stops as BUY liquidity |

**Application:** Check COT for each watchlist pair in the morning brief. Pairs with extreme positioning are the priority instruments — the sweep is coming for them.

## DHARMA PERIOD — THE SETUP WINDOW

**The first 15 minutes of any session open is NOT a trading window.**

It is the Dharma Period — the engineered setup. Institutions are building the trap, placing the false move, running early stops. If you trade the Dharma Period, you ARE the liquidity they are engineering against.

**Rule:** Mark. Observe. Do not execute in the first 15 minutes of London open or NY open.

The setup you identify during Dharma becomes the entry candidate AFTER the 15-minute window closes and the direction confirms.

## APPLYING LIQUIDITY ENGINEERING TO GRADE A CRITERION 3

**Criterion 3: Manipulation occurred (sweep/stop hunt at the key level)**

This is what "manipulation" actually means mechanically:
1. Identify the liquidity pool (equal highs/lows / PDH / PDL / round number / session extreme)
2. Price sweeps THROUGH the level (takes out stops)
3. Price CLOSES back through the level (rejection confirmed)
4. The sweep is the manipulation. The close back through is the signal.

**What to look for on the chart:**
- Wick extending past the level with close back through = sweep confirmed
- Volume spike on the sweep candle (institutions absorbing all those retail orders)
- Rapid reversal within 1-3 candles = institutional rejection

**What does NOT count:**
- Price approaching a level and turning before reaching it = no sweep
- Small wick, no close back through = indecision, not sweep
- Price breaking and holding above/below the level = genuine structural break
