## SECTION 1 — THE FOUR ASSET CLASSES AND THEIR HIERARCHY

The global financial market operates through four primary asset classes. They are not equal — they have a **hierarchy of information**, meaning some lead others in time.

```
HIERARCHY OF INFORMATION FLOW:

BONDS (1st) → fastest, most sophisticated participants
  ↓
CURRENCIES (2nd) → react to bond flows and yield differentials
  ↓
EQUITIES (3rd) → react to currency stability and credit conditions
  ↓
COMMODITIES (4th) → react to demand (equities) and currency (dollar)

→ The Sovereign reads bonds first. Always.
→ What bonds do today, currencies do tomorrow, equities do next week.
```

This is not theory. It is the mechanical reality of how capital moves through the system. Bond traders are the most sophisticated participants in the world — they operate on multi-year time horizons with the largest pools of capital. When they move, everything else follows.

## SECTION 4 — YIELD DIFFERENTIALS (THE FUNDAMENTAL ENGINE)

### What Drives Forex at the Deepest Level

At its most fundamental, a currency pair is a **yield differential trade**. Capital always flows toward the highest available risk-adjusted return. The interest rate of a country is the primary expression of that return.

**The simplest version:**
If the US 2-year Treasury yields 4.09% and the German 2-year Bund yields 2.5%, capital prefers US bonds → US dollar demand → EUR/USD falls (dollar stronger, euro weaker).

**The complete version:**
The yield differential between two countries, adjusted for:
1. **Central bank outlook** — is the spread likely to widen or narrow? (Future expected rate changes matter more than current rates)
2. **Currency risk premium** — is the higher-yielding currency at risk of devaluing and erasing the yield advantage?
3. **Liquidity premium** — is the bond market in that country liquid enough to enter and exit freely?

### The Key Yield Differentials to Track (Update Weekly)

| Pair | Yield Differential | How to Calculate | Where to Find |
|---|---|---|---|
| EUR/USD | US 2Y minus German 2Y Bund | fred.stlouisfed.org + ECB data | Wider = USD up, EUR down |
| USD/JPY | US 2Y minus Japan 2Y JGB | Most stretched differential in G7 | Wider = JPY down, USD up. Narrowing = violent JPY rally |
| GBP/USD | US 2Y minus UK 2Y Gilt | Bank of England data | UK Gilt yield rising → GBP can strengthen even vs USD |
| AUD/USD | Australia 2Y minus US 2Y | RBA data | Australia yield above US = AUD supported |
| USD/CAD | US 2Y minus Canada 2Y | Bank of Canada data | Canada rate above US = CAD supported |

### The USD/JPY Carry Trade — The Most Important Yield Story in Forex

The USD/JPY pair is, more than any other, a yield story. For 25+ years, Japan maintained near-zero interest rates while the US had positive rates. This created the world's largest **carry trade**:

```
THE CARRY TRADE MECHANISM:
1. Borrow Japanese Yen at ~0% interest rate (virtually free money)
2. Convert JPY to USD
3. Buy US Treasury bonds yielding 4-5%
4. Pocket the difference (the "carry") = free yield

Scale: Estimated $4+ trillion in global carry trades use JPY as the funding currency

THE UNWIND MECHANISM (the most dangerous event in forex):
If the BOJ raises rates (the JPY becomes more expensive to borrow):
→ Carry trade becomes less profitable
→ Institutional carry traders begin unwinding positions
→ They sell USD, buy JPY to repay the JPY loans
→ JPY appreciates sharply (sometimes 5-10% in days)
→ This forces OTHER carry traders to unwind (margin pressure)
→ A cascade begins
→ USD/JPY can fall 1,000+ pips in days during a full unwind
```

**Current read (May 2026):**
The BOJ is in the middle of its first rate normalization in decades. Every BOJ meeting is a potential carry-unwind trigger. With Uranus in Gemini (disrupting the ceremonial BOJ rate ritual of R7), a sudden policy shift is astronomically supported. The Sovereign treats every USD/JPY long position as carrying carry-unwind risk.

**The Sovereign's rule for USD/JPY:** Never hold a large long position without a hard stop that accounts for a 3-5% sudden JPY appreciation move. The upside on a carry trade is the slow drip of yield differential. The downside is a violent, fast crash.
