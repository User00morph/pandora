### NODE 1 — THE BIS (Bank for International Settlements)
**Location:** Basel, Switzerland
**Founded:** 1930
**Ray Signature:** Ray 1 (Will / Power) — the most concentrated expression of institutional will in the financial system

The BIS is the central bank of central banks. Every major national central bank (the Fed, ECB, BOJ, PBOC, Bank of England, Bank of Canada, RBA, SNB) is a member and reports to it. The BIS sets the framework — Basel Accords (Basel I, II, III) — that determines how much capital all commercial banks must hold, which directly controls the volume of credit (money) in the global system.

**What the BIS controls:**
- Global capital adequacy requirements (how much money can be created)
- Settlement standards for all cross-border transactions
- Foreign exchange reserve guidelines for member central banks
- The quarterly reporting cycle that drives institutional book-dressing

**Trading intelligence:** BIS quarterly reporting dates (March, June, September, December) create predictable institutional positioning behavior — balance sheet dressing and unwinding. Dollar funding typically spikes in the days before quarterly close. This is a repeating, tradeable pattern.

**Switzerland as the Nexus:** Switzerland hosts the BIS, the World Economic Forum (Davos — annual gathering of the high priesthood), the International Red Cross, and holds the largest number of private banking accounts globally. Its neutrality is structural — it is the safe harbor of the system, not separate from it.

### NODE 4 — THE IMF / WORLD BANK DEBT ARCHITECTURE
**Location:** Washington D.C.
**Ray Signature:** Ray 6 (Idealism / Devotion) expressed as narrative; Ray 1 underneath

The IMF and World Bank position themselves as development institutions — they claim to help struggling nations. The mechanism is: a nation enters financial distress → the IMF offers emergency liquidity → the liquidity comes with "structural adjustment conditions" → those conditions require privatization of state assets, currency liberalization, reduction of social programs, and market opening. The nation's sovereignty over its own economic policy is effectively transferred.

**Trading intelligence:** When the IMF announces emergency programs for a currency (Turkish Lira, Argentinian Peso, Egyptian Pound, Sri Lankan Rupee historically), it signals:
1. The currency has already experienced severe devaluation
2. IMF conditions will require further liberalization (more volatility)
3. The nation's central bank will lose effective policy independence
4. Debt repayment in USD will create sustained USD demand from that nation → pressure on their local currency

Emerging market currency crises (exotic pairs) almost always pass through an IMF phase. Reading where in that cycle a nation is tells you the trend direction for months or years.
