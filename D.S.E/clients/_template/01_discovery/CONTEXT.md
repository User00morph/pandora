# Stage 01 — Discovery
## Understand the actual problem. Define the scope.

## Purpose
The problem presented is rarely the problem that needs solving. Discovery surfaces the actual situation, identifies the real need, and produces a scope agreement that every subsequent stage works from.

## Inputs
- _config/client-brief.md (initial client communication and context)
- _config/engagement-terms.md (the structure of this engagement)

## Process
1. **Surface the actual problem.** Ask: what has been tried already? What would success look like in specific terms? What is the constraint — is it knowledge, time, resource, or something else?

2. **Identify failure modes.** What is the most likely way this engagement goes wrong? Name it explicitly. Build the scope to prevent it.

3. **Define scope clearly:**
   - What is included in this engagement?
   - What is explicitly excluded?
   - What does the final deliverable look like?
   - What does Morph need from the client to complete the work?

4. **Check against sovereign principles:**
   - Is this genuinely serviceable with current capacity?
   - Are the expectations realistic?
   - Are the terms sovereignty-preserving?

5. **Produce the scope agreement.** Both parties know exactly what is being delivered, when, and under what terms.

## Done looks like
A filed scope agreement with: deliverable defined, exclusions listed, client requirements listed, timeline agreed, and failure modes named. Morph and client both understand what "done" means before Stage 2 begins.

## Output goes to
`01_discovery/output/` — requirements doc + scope agreement → Stage 2 (Build)
