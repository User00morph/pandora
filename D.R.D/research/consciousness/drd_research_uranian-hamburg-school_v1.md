## 1. HISTORICAL ORIGIN

The Hamburg School of Astrology was formally established October 31, 1925 in Hamburg, Germany as the *Astrologenverein Hamburger Schule* (Astrological Association Hamburg School). Founded by **Alfred Witte** (1878–1941), a surveyor and amateur astronomer, the school developed through systematic observation of planetary midpoints and their correspondence with life events.

**Key figures:**
- **Alfred Witte:** Founder. Proposed four initial transsaturnian hypothetical points in the 1920s during collaborative astronomical searches (the period that also led to Pluto's discovery). His method involved retrograde-engineering hypothetical bodies from observed midpoint patterns in event charts.
- **Friedrich Sieggrün:** Co-developer. Added four more hypothetical points in 1927, completing the set of eight.
- **Ludwig Rudolph:** Preserved and reconstructed the system after World War II (the *Regelwerk* was among Nazi-burned books; Rudolph was imprisoned but ensured the system's survival). Re-founded the Hamburg School in 1947.
- **Reinhold Ebertin:** Student of the Hamburg School who created *Cosmobiology* — a stripped version eliminating the hypotheticals but preserving the midpoint doctrine and the 90°/45° dial system. Published COSI in 1940. The midpoint work already in SNRS v1 (Ebertin 45° sort) derives from this tradition.
- **Richard Svehla:** Introduced the Hamburg School to the United States in the 1930s. Coined the English name "Uranian Astrology."

**Historical disruption:** Witte allegedly died by suicide in 1941 to avoid Nazi concentration camp internment. The Uranian system survived through Rudolph's efforts and Svehla's American work.

## 2. CORE METHODOLOGY DISTINCTION

**The Hamburg School does not use the 12-house system.** Instead it works primarily with:

1. **Midpoints** — the mathematical midpoint between any two planets; the most sensitive point for triggering both planets simultaneously. Any planet or sensitive point at the midpoint of two others creates a *planetary picture*.

2. **Planetary Pictures (Planetenbilder)** — three-point combinations of the form A = B/C (A is at the midpoint of B and C). These are the primary interpretive unit of Uranian astrology.

3. **Dials** — specialized rotating overlays at 360°, 90°, and 45° scales. The 90° dial compresses the chart so that all four cardinal points (0° of each cardinal sign) overlap — revealing midpoint structures invisible in the standard wheel. The 45° dial further compresses this.

4. **Antiscia** — chart points at mirror positions across the 0° Cancer/Capricorn axis. Already included in SNRS v2 A6 — this is a Hamburg School technique.

5. **The eight hypothetical transsaturnian bodies** (see below).

**Key distinction from mainstream astrology:** The Hamburg School is less interested in sign rulership and house placement than in midpoint structures and planetary pictures. It reads the chart algebraically (what + what = what) rather than symbolically by placement.

**Bridge to SNRS:** The Ebertin 45° midpoint sort already in SNRS v1 is the Cosmobiology offshoot of this system. Adding the eight hypotheticals completes the Witte-Ebertin lineage that SNRS is already partially using.

## 3. THE EIGHT HYPOTHETICAL PLANETS

These are mathematical points computed from specific orbital elements, not observed physical bodies. The Swiss Ephemeris includes all eight (SE_CUPIDO = 40 through SE_POSEIDON = 47) using "Witte/Sieggrün planets, refined by James Neely."

Their nature: possibly barycenters of planetoid belts, or tuning forks for harmonic frequencies — the tradition does not insist on their physical reality but on their functional correspondence.

### CUPIDO (Witte's 1st hypothetical)
**Archetype:** Community, partnerships, art, marriage, groups, the family-beyond-blood, artistic unions, collective belonging  
**Key themes:** Where Cupido is active, questions of *belonging* and *creative companionship* arise — the group, the collective, the shared aesthetic endeavor. Not purely romantic love (that's Venus) but the principle of *union in purpose*.  
**Orbital period:** ~262 years  
**Approximate position May 12, 2006:** ~8°–10° Sagittarius (requires precise pyswisseph computation)  
**Midpoint keyword:** Community/art/marriage. Planetary picture with any point = "group engagement" or "artistic partnership" flavor.

### HADES (Witte's 2nd hypothetical)
**Archetype:** Decay, depth, the ancient, the hidden, the degenerate, esoteric vocations, healing work, the underworld, ancestral residue  
**Key themes:** Hades does not signify evil — it signifies the *layer beneath the surface*. Where Hades operates: hidden matters, ancient things, work with the neglected or unseen, healing through confrontation with decay. Esoteric and therapeutic vocations are strongly Hades-marked.  
**Distinction from Pluto:** Pluto transforms; Hades reveals what was already hidden. Pluto is volcanic; Hades is archaeological.  
**Orbital period:** ~360 years  
**Approximate position May 12, 2006:** ~9°–11° Libra (requires precise computation)

### ZEUS (Witte's 3rd hypothetical)
**Archetype:** Controlled power, fire, creativity-with-precision, ambitious achievement, fertility, military force directed by intelligence, leadership with strategy  
**Key themes:** Zeus is NOT Jupiter (uncontained expansion). Zeus is directed fire — the arrow aimed, the campaign planned, the creative force organized toward a specific achievement. Where Zeus operates: purposeful ambition, strategic leadership, precision-controlled creativity.  
**Orbital period:** ~455 years  
**Approximate position May 12, 2006:** ~1°–3° Libra (requires precise computation)

### KRONOS (Witte's 4th hypothetical)
**Archetype:** Authority, mastery, height, government, kings and leaders, the highest of its kind, standards of excellence, law, the elevated vantage point  
**Key themes:** Kronos (distinct from Saturn) is about *the peak* — the highest expression, the governing standard. Where Kronos operates: matters of authority, expertise, elevation, the best of its category. Kronos conjunct a natal planet elevates that planet's function to its highest possible expression.  
**Distinction from Saturn:** Saturn is the discipline that builds structure over time. Kronos is the *achieved standard*, the height that discipline has reached.  
**Orbital period:** ~521 years  
**Approximate position May 12, 2006:** ~28°–29° Gemini (requires precise computation)

**Key themes:** Apollon is a higher-octave of Jupiter but with a specifically *intellectual and connective* quality. Where Apollon operates: far-reaching networks, broad wisdom, commerce at a large scale, the ability to hold many things simultaneously without confusion. Prophetic and synthetic.

## 5. COMPUTATION NOTE

All positions listed above are approximate, derived from known orbital periods and reference positions. **Precise computation requires running pyswisseph with SE_CUPIDO through SE_POSEIDON (bodies 40–47) and SE_TRANSPLUTO (body 48) for date May 12, 2006, 08:07 UT-5 (13:07 UT), 44.5133°N / 88.0158°W.**

import swisseph as swe
