## 1. DOCTRINE

The **Progressed Lunation Cycle** tracks the ongoing relationship between the **secondary progressed Moon** and the **secondary progressed Sun**. As both bodies continue their symbolic motion after birth (one day after birth = one year of life), they form an ever-changing angular relationship — a cycle that completes approximately every **29–30 years**.

This cycle was systematized by **Dane Rudhyar** as a map of *psychological and spiritual development* — not external events (transits cover those) but the inner arc of becoming: what phase of development the soul is navigating at any given time.

## 2. THE EIGHT PHASES

The 360° progressed Sun-Moon cycle is divided into 8 phases of 45° each.

**Phase angle = progressed Moon longitude - progressed Sun longitude (if negative, add 360°)**

---

### PHASE 1 — NEW MOON (0°–45°)
**Quality:** Beginning, seed, impulse, identity crystallization, subjective awareness
**Inner experience:** A new cycle is starting but its shape is not yet visible. The person is operating from instinct and impulse rather than clarity. There is tremendous energy but it is undifferentiated — the seed has not yet broken ground. Actions taken feel necessary without being fully understood.
**Challenge:** Difficulty seeing objectively; the self is so immersed in the new beginning that perspective is limited
**Duration:** Approximately 3.7 years

---

### PHASE 2 — CRESCENT (45°–90°)
**Quality:** Assertion, struggle, breaking free from the past, building momentum against resistance
**Inner experience:** The new impulse meets the residue of the old cycle. What the Balsamic phase prepared to release is still present as resistance. The Crescent phase demands assertion and conscious effort — the new growth has to push up against what was. Energy is available but requires direction.
**Challenge:** The pull of the old is still strong; the new intention must be deliberately maintained against the gravity of familiar patterns
**Duration:** Approximately 3.7 years

---

### PHASE 3 — FIRST QUARTER (90°–135°)
**Quality:** Crisis of action, building the external structure, decisive movement, manifestation of the impulse into form
**Inner experience:** The new cycle's impulse must now create something real in the world — it is not enough to intend or to assert. The First Quarter demands *action*, specifically action that builds external structure. A crisis of commitment: the person must choose and act, or the impulse dissipates.
**Rudhyar's formulation:** "Crisis in action" — the challenge is not intellectual but executive. What will you DO with what you know you need to become?
**Duration:** Approximately 3.7 years

---

### PHASE 4 — GIBBOUS (135°–180°)
**Quality:** Refinement, technique-building, the last preparation before fullness, overcoming obstacles through skill
**Inner experience:** The structure being built (First Quarter) must now be *refined* — the rough draft of the new cycle is being polished into something ready for the Full Moon's illumination. This is the phase of technique, craft, and the final preparation. Something is nearly complete. The person may feel impatience (so close to fullness) or intense focus on getting the details right.
**Rudhyar's formulation:** "Overcoming" — the obstacles encountered are now being resolved through skill and perseverance
**Duration:** Approximately 3.7 years

---

### PHASE 5 — FULL MOON (180°–225°)
**Quality:** Fulfillment, illumination, objectivity, the peak of awareness, seeing oneself in relation to others
**Inner experience:** The cycle reaches its peak expression. What was seeded at the New Moon is now fully visible — illuminated. The Full Moon phase brings the capacity for objectivity: the self can see itself clearly in relationship to others, to the collective, to the purpose the cycle has been building. There may be a sense of culmination, achievement, or of finally understanding what the long development has been for.
**Challenge:** The Full Moon is also the point of maximum tension — full oppositions, maximum visibility, the self under scrutiny. The light reveals everything, including what hasn't been resolved.
**Duration:** Approximately 3.7 years

---

### PHASE 6 — DISSEMINATING (225°–270°)
**Quality:** Sharing, spreading, teaching, contributing what was learned to the collective
**Inner experience:** After the Full Moon's illumination, the task shifts from receiving to *giving*. The insights, achievements, and understanding from the cycle's development are now meant to flow outward. This is the phase of the teacher, the communicator, the bridge-builder who takes what was learned and makes it useful for others.
**Challenge:** The person may cling to what they've achieved rather than releasing it into the world; or may overextend by trying to spread too many things at once
**Duration:** Approximately 3.7 years

---

### PHASE 7 — LAST QUARTER (270°–315°)
**Quality:** Crisis of consciousness, reorientation, questioning, dismantling what no longer serves
**Inner experience:** What served the cycle's purpose is now becoming obsolete. The Last Quarter brings a *crisis of consciousness* — not a crisis of action (First Quarter) but a crisis of understanding and values. Beliefs, structures, and commitments formed early in the cycle are being questioned. What needs to go? What needs to be reevaluated before the next New Moon begins?
**Rudhyar's formulation:** "Crisis in consciousness" — the external harvest of Disseminating is complete; now the internal frameworks must be dismantled and reformed
**Duration:** Approximately 3.7 years

---

### PHASE 8 — BALSAMIC (315°–360°)
**Quality:** Surrender, seed-forming, release, the future in potential, visionary access
**Inner experience:** The end of the cycle. The Balsamic phase is the most spiritually intense and the most releasing. What was built in the entire 30-year arc is being composted into the seed of the next cycle. The person may feel weary, visionary, detached from personal ambition, drawn toward spiritual meaning. There is an initiatory quality — Balsamic people often carry a quality of having "seen something" that others haven't reached yet.
**Birth in Balsamic phase:** Being born during a Balsamic natal phase (Moon 315°-360° ahead of the Sun) is traditionally associated with a dharmic emphasis — the soul arriving near the end of a large arc, carrying forward something from a previous cycle.
**Duration:** Approximately 3.7 years

## 3. COMPUTATION METHOD

**Step 1:** Identify natal Sun and Moon longitudes
**Step 2:** Apply secondary progression formula (day-for-year): for age N, the progressed Sun position = natal Sun + N degrees (approximately); progressed Moon position = natal Moon + (N × mean daily Moon motion ÷ 1 year)

Note: The progressed Moon's actual daily motion varies (the Moon's orbital speed is not constant). Swiss Ephemeris calculates this precisely. Mean estimate: ~13.2° per year of life.

**Step 3:** Phase angle = progressed Moon - progressed Sun (add 360 if negative)
**Step 4:** Match to the 8-phase table above
