## 1. DOCTRINE

Firdaria (plural: firdariat; from Arabic *fardār*, from Persian/Pahlavi origins) is a time-lord system that divides the human lifespan into sequential planetary periods. It is one of several time-lord techniques in Persian and Arabic astrology — parallel to Zodiacal Releasing and Profections but operating on a different timing logic.

**Core principle:** Each planet rules a *major period* of a defined number of years. Within each major period, every other planet rules a *sub-period* proportional to its own period length. The governing planets at any given moment — the *major lord* and the *sub-lord* — are the archetypal forces most actively shaping the person's life chapter.

Unlike Zodiacal Releasing (which is sign-based) or Profections (which advance one sign per year), Firdaria is **planet-based and sect-based** — the sequence and its starting planet are determined by whether the chart is a Day chart or Night chart.

## 2. SECT DETERMINATION

**Sect = Day or Night** is determined by the Sun's position relative to the horizon at the moment of birth.

- **Day chart:** Sun is ABOVE the horizon (in houses 7–12, i.e., in the upper hemisphere of the chart)
- **Night chart:** Sun is BELOW the horizon (in houses 1–6, i.e., in the lower hemisphere of the chart)

The horizon is the Ascendant-Descendant axis. A planet in houses 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, or 12 is above the horizon. Houses 1–6 are below.

**Day chart sequence:** Sun begins the Firdaria  
**Night chart sequence:** Moon begins the Firdaria

## 4. SEQUENCE BY SECT

### Day Chart Sequence
Sun → Venus → Mercury → Moon → Saturn → Jupiter → Mars → North Node → South Node → (repeat)

### Night Chart Sequence
Moon → Saturn → Jupiter → Mars → Sun → Venus → Mercury → North Node → South Node → (repeat)

**The sequence logic:** In a Day chart, the diurnal luminary (Sun) initiates. In a Night chart, the nocturnal luminary (Moon) initiates. The remaining sequence follows a specific traditional order that interleaves the sect planets (Day sect: Sun, Jupiter, Saturn; Night sect: Moon, Venus, Mars) with Mercury (common to both sects, placed at the end of the main sequence before the Nodes).

## 5. SUB-PERIOD STRUCTURE

Within each major planetary period, sub-periods are assigned to each planet in the same sequence (beginning with the major lord itself).

**Sub-period length formula:**
```
Sub-period length = (sub-planet's major period / 75) × major lord's major period
```

**Example: Within a Saturn major period (11 years), the Saturn sub-period is:**
(11 / 75) × 11 = 1.613 years ≈ 1 year 7 months 11 days

**Example: Within a Jupiter major period (12 years), the Jupiter sub-period is:**
(12 / 75) × 12 = 1.92 years ≈ 1 year 11 months

## 6. INTERPRETATION PRINCIPLES

### Major Lord
The major Firdaria lord is the **dominant archetypal governor** of the life chapter. It determines the overall *quality* of the period — what kind of experiences are most active, what themes dominate, what planet's agenda the person is working through.

**Interpreting the major lord:**
- Identify the planet's natal placement: sign, house, dignity
- Identify its natal aspects: what planets does it connect to?
- The major period will foreground the natal themes of this planet
- The planet's natal strength (dignity, angularity) determines whether the period is constructive or challenging
- A well-placed major lord = a productive chapter; a debilitated major lord = a chapter requiring difficult work

### Sub-Lord
The sub-lord *colors* the major period and provides more granular timing. The sub-lord's natal condition and its relationship to the major lord (by natal aspect) determine how it modifies the major period's energy.

- **Major lord and sub-lord in harmonious natal aspect** → sub-period flows with the major period
- **Major lord and sub-lord in challenging natal aspect** → sub-period introduces friction within the broader chapter
- **Sub-lord well-placed natally** → sub-period has its own productive momentum
- **Sub-lord debilitated natally** → sub-period brings its specific challenges within the chapter

### Nodes
The North Node period is traditionally associated with increase, gain, and opening. The South Node period is associated with decrease, release, and completion. Both Nodes are shorter than planetary periods (3 and 2 years respectively) — they function as transitional passages between the longer planetary epochs.

## 7. HISTORICAL CONTEXT

Firdaria originated in Persian/Sassanid astrology and was transmitted into the Arabic tradition through the Abbasid-era translation movement (8th–9th centuries). Abu Mashar (787–886 CE) is the primary Arabic systematizer. The system was transmitted to medieval Europe through Latin translations of Arabic texts.

The word *fardār* (فردار) may derive from the Greek *prorogator* (time-releaser) via Persian transmission, though the exact etymology is contested. The system is distinct from the Greek Aphesis system but shares the same impulse: identifying which planet is "releasing" (prorogating) time and therefore governing life events.

Firdaria fell out of mainstream Western astrology during the 17th–18th centuries along with other Hellenistic and Persian techniques. Its revival belongs to the late-20th century reconstructionist movement — principally through the work of Robert Zoller, Robert Hand, and later Benjamin Dykes and Chris Brennan.
