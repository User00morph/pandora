## PART I: SIGN SECTIONS

### CANCER (ASC + Mars sign)
*(Book pp. 93–95, PDF pp. 111–113)*

Cancer is the most personal of the Water signs. Cancerian children respond to their environment so intensely they are among the most sensitive in the universe. The ruling planet is the Moon — the "great mood changer." This is a Cardinal sign, full of energy and possessing great ambition, though the driving ambition is difficult to see because of the emotional tensions.

It has been said that Cancerians are the orphans of the zodiac — more Cancerian children than any other sign have lost a mother or father. **(This also happens when the Moon is in Cancer or when Cancer is the Ascendant.)** Even when both parents are present, they feel emotionally orphaned or emotionally deprived.

Cancer arrives in a mother-dominated world. The mother rules the marriage at the time of the child's birth. Sometimes it seems to Cancer that mother rules the universe because the father is missing. The child doesn't know why he's absent — Cancer only knows that mothers do everything. The mother is described as highly manipulative; she controls people around her by acting the role of mother surrogate or by acting the role of helpless little girl. The child sees this and grows up to act just like Mom — playing Big Momma/Big Daddy or Helpless Little Girl/Boy. Cancerian types have little experience playing the role of adult men or women.

The Cancerian female presents herself as a helpless little thing who needs protection. She marries a man who will save her from her environment. But she develops into a very strong woman. She may call her husband at the office twenty times a day; or pull scenes if he arrives home late. She may become a parasite, looking for constant reassurance. She learned through observing her mother that one must control men, manipulate them gently, in order to get what one wants. She has a lot of "gimme" in her personality until she matures.

The Cancerian female becomes quite aggressive in the career area. The Cancerian type is not the homebody we hear about — she prefers a nice home available to her, but has to be out in the world exploring and feeling with the masses. She does well in career once she settles into something that interests her. Many Cancerian women have jobs that include travel. She looks for executive power — that is one way to use the Great Mother energy constructively.

The Cancerian male has more trouble getting free of his early childhood environment. He realizes women are strong and powerful, and adopts his mother's mannerisms and attitudes. He may either be too emotionally close to his mother — or really dislike her and later make women in his life pay the dues for his mother hatred. He usually prefers the company of women; enters relationships playing the role of Big Daddy or Little Boy. He, too, is looking for emotional fulfillment and affection — something akin to "mother love" more than a sexual relationship.

The Cancerian man may not be quite as interested in career as the Cancerian woman — the motivation for accomplishment usually comes from impetus elsewhere in the chart. He can do well on the job if he so desires — playing father surrogate (Big Daddy to all people in his office) or Little Boy (a salesman who makes lots of sales because the Little Boy role doesn't threaten authority). If he has problems holding a job, it's usually because he's too much of a rebel. Mother-dominated signs have little respect for male authority figures. They need freedom on the job, and need to be interested in what they are doing.

Cancer is the sign that indicates nurturing and growth. Both males and females have the capability to teach and train, to encourage and help others. They make good teachers, can be helpful to people in general. They are moody and intuitive — they function much better listening to intuition rather than intellect. Because no one paid attention to what Cancerian children said at home, it can become very important for them to get a college education, for the "grades" prove to the world they really are as smart as everyone else. They are often put down as children since they have emotional reactions to what goes on rather than "rational" reactions. *However, the emotional reaction is the start of the intuitive process — and the rest of the signs have to take courses to reopen the channels of the intuitive process!* Cancerians respond better to feelings than to words. Ask them how they feel about something rather than what they think.

**Planets in Cancer** pick up its sensitivity and intuitive quality. Mars in Cancer tends to act from an emotional point; Venus in Cancer is a loving and sensual but emotionally insecure person; Saturn in Cancer takes emotional security seriously — is concerned about security, fears emotional involvements, or feels a lack of sensitivity or intuition.

### SCORPIO (Moon + Jupiter sign)
*(Book pp. 96–98, PDF pp. 114–116)*

Scorpio children are born in a mother-dominated atmosphere. They do not come into an environment that is warm and loving, and a parent may be missing. There is often a death in the family a few days before a Scorpio is born — this affects the emotional set of the parents, bringing an attitude of grief into the small child's life. Often Scorpios are unwanted children. Or they are just one more child in a family that can't afford another mouth to feed.

Scorpios born in this type of atmosphere pick up all the unpleasant vibrations. The infant Scorpio needs lots of attention and love, but the father ignores the child and the mother often gives only minimum care: food, shelter, diaper changes. There is little affection, warmth, and holding. Later, the parents feel guilty and try to make it up by spoiling him or providing many opportunities in life. But the little Scorpio isn't buying it. The child needs affection early in life — the allowance and extra privileges he gets later on don't mean anything.

Because of the early experience, **Scorpios are on a life-long search for love and affection. They search for the mother love that was missed in childhood.** Scorpios have a reputation as the "super sexed" sign, but they aren't as sexy as they would have us think. The young Scorpio male and female confuse sex with love: "If you have sex with someone they will love you." So they learn how to be good at sex — but people who have a relationship with Scorpios often find that after the relationship has been established, the sexual part of it is not nearly as important as it was. **Affection becomes more important to Scorpios.**

When young Scorpios feel insecure, they try to create sexual situations — it makes them feel secure when they think someone "loves" them. If you love an immature Scorpio and are perceptive, you may feel that sex for them is not merely sensual pleasure but is used to control you.

Scorpios are born to go through **great personal transformations.** The life is such that either they spend a lifetime trying to create a family experience for themselves, or they go into the business of self-transformation. The problem here is direction — often Scorpios try both. They want to manage a tight household and transform the world at the same time. Because they felt unaccepted in early childhood, **acceptance becomes important to them.** In the attempt to become accepted, confusion arises when they try to lay down the law to others and control the behavior of others.

Scorpio career: they can go in many directions. They often choose corporate structure — which is like a family for them; the new-found family can approve of them, promote them, and they belong. Usually the corporate type of Scorpio is indicating a need for father approval, which may mean the father was either harsh or quite distant. They are also drawn to scientific and social research — investigative minds can be put to good use. Detective or police work, analysis of any kind is good for this sign. There is an interest in the medical profession — this field brings out the research abilities as well as the transformational or healing quality.

The Scorpio female grows up to be strong and secretive like her mother, and she often copies her mother's mannerisms. This gives her magnetism and an air of secrecy. She carries within her, however, a feeling of personal insecurity, a sense of worthlessness that can only be assuaged by getting into love relationships. She can use her sexuality for power. She gives herself sexually until she has what she wants under control — and then she stops. The person she relates to feels confounded. Sometimes the young Scorpio female marries a man who treats her badly because she thinks she doesn't deserve more than that. In her search for a strong man, she manipulates — magnetic, like the female spider in her web. But if he turns out to be too powerful, if she can't control him, she may get so uncomfortable she leaves him.

The Scorpio male carries a fantastic sexual reputation from all the dime-store astrology books, and he doesn't feel sexy inside. He wants a love relationship that includes plenty of mother love and affection. He confuses sex with love, and finds it difficult to return the love given to him since he saw little of it as a child. He feels uncomfortable admitting that he loves someone — he thinks this leaves him open for attack.

Seldom is the Scorpio male child not criticized and put down by someone in his family. The secrecy that Scorpio projects is a cover for the fear of rejection. The Scorpio child is extremely intuitive and "knowing" — and he is laughed at or rejected when he tries to share his thoughts. Rather than face more rejection, he learns to keep his thoughts to himself and grows into the tall, dark "fascinating" stranger.

Both male and female of this sign have **tension-related illnesses.** The Scorpio personality is so moody, so emotional, so sensitive, so intuitive, and so afraid to share emotion for fear of rejection, that all these feelings are pushed down inside and churn around in the digestive and intestinal tracts. As Scorpios hit their forties, the tension begins to tell — more prone to high-tension disorders or intestinal and digestive stress diseases.

They need to learn to share. As Scorpios learn to share their ideas and dreams and hopes, they begin to find out if anyone really likes them. It's so important to Scorpios to have meaningful and intense relationships that it's more profitable to be open in the long run. They draw people who are impressed with their "mysterious" act, but their true selves are never really known. It is far more rewarding to be open and be either rejected or accepted.

**Planets in Scorpio** pick up some of the intensity and magnetism of the sign. Venus in Scorpio is not easy — the Scorpio child feels unwanted, therefore Venus in Scorpio wants to feel unwanted — setup for the masochist. Mars in Scorpio acts in a searching manner — as an investigator, or unwanted. Saturn in Scorpio takes rejection seriously, takes research seriously. These are the people who like to build families.

## PART II: ASPECT READINGS

### Aspect Framework (Lundsted's Approach)
*(Book pp. 104–106, PDF pp. 122–124)*

- **Conjunction:** "More of" — each planet's function colored by the other
- **Sextile:** Easy, but can indicate undeveloped talent — must be developed
- **Square:** One of the most difficult — frustration; two parts of personality at war internally; stumbling blocks that can be rechanneled into stepping stones
- **Trine:** Easiest — may be taken for granted, not consciously developed; can indicate element emphasis that becomes its own problem
- **Opposition:** Compromise required; tension between two facets of self
- **Quincunx:** Strain — classic health aspect; two ideas conflicting, not as visible as square; if not understood, the body rebels

**Orbs used:** Conjunction/trine/square/opposition: 10°; sextile: 6°; quincunx: 5°; Sun or Moon involved: may stretch to 15° for hard aspects

### SUN-NEPTUNE SQUARE
*(Book pp. 141–142, PDF pp. 159–160)*

Here the child is born into an atmosphere that requires spiritual development. The father tries to develop spiritually, tries to find himself in the universe, but seems to miss out on the spiritual awareness that is necessary for his own particular form of spiritual growth. Perhaps his interest in religion is rather perverse, and his religious trip (or anti-religion trip) prohibits him from developing as he needs to.

The father of a Sun square Neptune child may be a "holy-roller" — one of those people who gives up smiling and loving and compassion in the pursuit of his God. The God-consciousness can develop any way that it wishes. If the father got lost on some fanatic trip, or if he got lost on some atheistic trip, this person may assume that all interest in religion or philosophy must be "crazy." If the father was an atheist, the person may be afraid to pursue his interest in religious development.

**People with this aspect are often presented with an image of the father that is not altogether accurate.** When parents divorce, a mother may tell her children that she ended the marriage because the father was incompetent. The truth may be that she drove the man out. When there is a Neptune-Sun aspect, it's wise to become suspicious of the mother's reports of the father. He was either better or worse than she describes — he may have been listening to a different drummer.

Parents can influence or confuse this child's personal concept of reality more than they realize. The Sun-Neptune child is intuitive but this intuition may not be trusted. They lack perspective — their aspirations may be muddled because the foundation of the personality is not understood.

The Sun represents our essential spirit — the part that enlivens the body. The Neptune affliction can blind one to the essence of the personal structure. The aspect can be worked through as the individual realizes that many conscious memories have been "fed" to him, that he must establish a way of life for himself in the conscious sphere. **Once this is accomplished, the energy within can be used on a constructive level. This energy can be extremely creative.**

### MOON-JUPITER CONJUNCTION
*(Book p. 167, PDF p. 185)*

This conjunction often indicates someone who relates effusively in the manner described by the sign involved in the conjunction. The mother of a child with this aspect is **indulgent** — she indulges in emotional reactions, she indulges her body in some way. The child will too. This indicates a person who may have a close relationship with the mother, who may stay close to her long after other siblings have left home.

This may be a person who **indulges in the physical appetites and therefore overdoes in some way** — too much food, alcohol, etc.

**This individual is open emotionally, open to feeling each new experience, open to relating how he feels to those he knows, open to sharing what he has.**

This conjunction often denotes a generous emotional nature — how the generosity will operate will be determined by the sign as well as the other aspects to the conjunction. This person may want to do so much for the mother that he never leaves home to develop a personal life.

*[In Scorpio: this conjunction's generous emotional nature runs through the Scorpio filter — the search for deep acceptance, the transformational drive, the magnetism. Open to relating while simultaneously guarded against rejection.]*

**MOON-URANUS TRINE:** The child born with this aspect comes into a home where the mother understands the attitudes of the generation when the child is small. Uranus indicates the behavior of the generation; also our point of eccentricity or willfulness. **This child's mother is willing to work with her environment in a positive manner, and her children will imitate her creative response to life in their adult relationships.**

The only problem from this trine can be too much permissiveness — perhaps too much support given to people before understanding their goals and purposes.

**This aspect can be called the aspect of "positive" eccentricity!** It indicates that the individual has a creative bent that bestows an ability to be emotionally involved in any career chosen. It also indicates an unusual emotional nature, one that may enjoy "new age" relationships.

### MERCURY-JUPITER OPPOSITION
*(Book pp. 199–200, PDF p. 217)*

**MERCURY-JUPITER ASPECTS introduction:** When these two planets are linked, the ability to use the senses, to communicate, to use the mind, is combined with the relating principle. Jupiter represents how we absorb the atmosphere around us, how open we are to receiving new life experience. Mercury can indicate how we listen. During the Hero's venture (the process of individuation), we must be able to read the signs along the road.

**MERCURY-JUPITER OPPOSITION:** This person comes from a childhood in which the family doesn't relate to him. The power of the trine can be developed out of an opposition, but constructive use of the energy will have to be learned — early childhood experience doesn't provide the training.

Friends and opportunities may be lost because of a poor choice of words. The opposition causes a compromise between the relating ability and the need for communication. It can express in an excessive manner — one day relating nicely, the next not; or words can come out in a tirade. The feeling is that if communication is honest (according to the sign Mercury is in), then relationships can't happen. These feelings create inner tensions; eventually resentments develop because real communication isn't achieved.

**The aspect is a powerful one. The resentments will go once the individual learns how to express the compromise in a profitable manner. This can be learned once he realizes that he was not shown how to do it by his family.**

For example, Mercury in Aries, Jupiter in Libra: the Aries Mercury is outspoken, idealistic, helpful of the "underdog." Jupiter in Libra wants to relate in a beautiful and socially acceptable manner to only the "right" people. The Mercury in Aries needs to learn diplomacy, and Jupiter in Libra needs to leave some room for ideals.

## PART III: SYNTHESIS NOTES

### Grand Water Trine in Lundsted's Framework
The entire Grand Water Trine (Mars-Cancer / Jupiter-Moon-Scorpio / Uranus-Pisces) is made of trines that Lundsted reads as fundamentally constructive but requiring conscious development. The trines are not automatic — they are **stored potential that must be activated through lived experience**:

- Mars-Jupiter trine → encouraged action, can relate to what one does
- Mars-Uranus trine → mark of genius, needs Sun/Moon expression to release
- Jupiter-Uranus trine → philosophical leadership potential, "thoroughly tested"
- Moon-Uranus trine → creative bent, "positive eccentricity," unusual emotional nature
- Moon-Mars trine → emotionally receptive, healthy relationships, actions aligned with feelings

The trine stellium suggests profound ease in the Water element — but Water trines in Lundsted's framework carry the risk of being taken for granted, or never consciously developed. The genius remains latent until the individual decides to work it.

### Mercury Kite in Lundsted's Framework
Mercury is the apex planet of the Kite — it opposes Jupiter/Moon (difficulty expressing openly; relating and communicating at cross-purposes; resentments released through honest communication) and sextiles Uranus (creative innovative talent, unconventional communication, may not manifest until after 30).

Lundsted's consistent message: the sextile indicates **talent that must be developed**. The opposition indicates **patterns that were not modeled in childhood and must be consciously learned**. The Mercury Kite is therefore a diagram of: natural creative talent (sextile-Uranus, sextile-Mars) blocked by an unlearned communication pattern (opposition-Jupiter) — the resolution is learning what the family didn't teach.

### Scorpio Moon-Jupiter Conjunction
The Moon-Jupiter conjunction in Scorpio amplifies everything Lundsted says about Scorpio: the search for mother love, the confusion of sex with love, the magnetism, the fear of rejection. Jupiter conjunct Moon **amplifies** these needs — adds indulgence, effusiveness, the inability to moderate. Combined with Scorpio's intensity, this is either a person of extraordinary emotional depth who transforms those they touch, or someone whose emotional needs overwhelm both self and partner until the lessons are consciously integrated.

### Sun-Neptune Square
The father image distorted, incomplete, or spiritually confused. The child's concept of "what is real" has been influenced in some way — aspirations muddled. **The correction: establishing one's own foundation in the conscious sphere.** Once this is done, the Sun-Neptune square becomes extremely creative. This squares Morph's Gemini Sun from Pisces Neptune — mental flexibility meets oceanic dissolution; the square is the friction that eventually produces the pearl.
