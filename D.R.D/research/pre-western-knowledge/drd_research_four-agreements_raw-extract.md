## ORIGIN TRACE

**The Toltec:**
"Toltec" derives from Nahuatl — *toltecatl* — meaning "people of knowledge" or "artisan." The Toltec were a Mesoamerican civilization centered at Tula (Tollan) in central Mexico. They were recognized across Mesoamerica as masters of science, art, and spiritual knowledge. Their influence extended to the Maya and Aztec civilizations. The nagual (also spelled nahual) is a specific role within indigenous Mesoamerican tradition — a shaman or spiritual leader capable of perceiving and moving between layers of reality.

**Don Miguel Ruiz:**
Born into a family of Toltec healers in rural Mexico. His mother was a curandera (healer), his grandfather a nagual. He trained as a medical surgeon at UNAM. A near-death car accident redirected him toward the ancestral teachings of his family's lineage. He has taught internationally since the 1980s. The Four Agreements was published in 1997 and has sold over 10 million copies globally.

**The Core Premise:**
Ruiz presents reality as a collectively agreed-upon "dream" — a shared hallucination that each culture and individual maintains through unconscious agreements absorbed during childhood (what he calls "domestication"). These agreements — received through punishment and reward — define our identity, our limits, and our suffering. The Four Agreements are the counter-agreements: conscious replacements for the unconscious ones.

## THE FOUR AGREEMENTS — RAW TEXT POSITIONS

**Agreement 1: Be Impeccable with Your Word**
"Speak with integrity. Say only what you mean. Avoid using the word to speak against yourself or to gossip about others. Use the power of your word in the direction of truth and love."

Root: *Impeccable* — from Latin *impeccabilis* — without sin, without fault. In Ruiz's framing, "sin" is anything that goes against yourself. The word is treated as the most powerful creative force humans possess. A word spoken becomes a seed — it creates. An impeccable word plants what you intend. A corrupt word plants what you did not intend and then must manage.

**Agreement 2: Don't Take Anything Personally**
"Nothing others do is because of you. What others say and do is a projection of their own reality, their own dream. When you are immune to the opinions and actions of others, you won't be the victim of needless suffering."

Root: Personal importance (*personalismo*) is identified as maximum selfishness — the assumption that everything is about you. All projection outward is the projector's dream, not objective reality. Agreement 2 is a firewall: it prevents external projections from becoming internal agreements.

**Agreement 3: Don't Make Assumptions**
"Find the courage to ask questions and to express what you really want. Communicate with others as clearly as you can to avoid misunderstandings, sadness, and drama. With just this one agreement, you can completely transform your life."

Root: Assumptions are the mind filling unknown space with projected meaning — usually negative, usually self-referential. Ruiz identifies three destructive assumption patterns: (1) assuming we know what someone means without asking, (2) assuming others think as we do, (3) assuming others will respond as we would. Each creates suffering when reality does not match the assumption.

**Agreement 4: Always Do Your Best**
"Your best is going to change from moment to moment; it will be different when you are healthy as opposed to sick. Under any circumstance, simply do your best, and you will avoid self-judgment, self-abuse, and regret."

Root: The best is not a fixed performance standard — it is a relational standard. The best today with full energy differs from the best today ill. What matters is that the full capacity of the present moment is brought to the present action. This dissolves self-punishment (you did not meet yesterday's standard) and complacency (you stopped before your actual current capacity).
