## Key Definitions Read On-Air

**Fiduciary (Wikipedia / Black's Law):**
- Person who holds a legal or ethical relationship of trust with one or more other parties
- Prudently takes care of money or other assets for another person
- Financial advisors, planners, asset managers = fiduciaries under applicable statutes
- In a fiduciary relationship, one person in a position of vulnerability vests confidence, good faith, reliance, and trust in another
- Good conscience requires the fiduciary to act at all times for the sole benefit and interest of the one who trusts

**Fiduciary Duty:**
- Highest standard of care in equity or law
- Fiduciary must be extremely loyal to the principal (you)
- No conflict between fiduciary duty and fiduciary's personal interest
- Fiduciary must not profit from their position without the principal's consent
- "Fiduciaries must conduct themselves at a level higher than that trodden by the crowd"
- "The distinguishing or overriding duty of a fiduciary is the obligation of undivided loyalty" — Lord Millett, Bristol and West Building Society v Mothew

**Equity jurisdiction:**
- Fiduciary relationship = concept within equity (not common law)
- UK Judicature Acts merged courts of equity (Court of Chancery) with common law courts
- Fiduciary duty standard is STRICTER than the comparable tortious duty of care in common law
