## 4. STAGE 3 — EVIDENCE LOG

**JIANG (Tier 3) — Game Theory of Coordination:**
Claim: In a multi-player winner-take-all game, the mathematically optimal strategy is secret coordination via transgression, which creates self-enforcing group cohesion through mutual blackmail.
Limitation: Presented as established game theory but is actually a synthesized theory — not a formally published academic result. The transgression-cohesion relationship is plausible but not empirically tested as stated.
Contradiction: NONE with basic game theory principles (the logic of the prisoner's dilemma, coalition formation, credible commitment devices). The specific transgression mechanism goes beyond standard game theory but is logically consistent with it.

**PIKETTY (Tier 2) — Financialization:**
Claim: Financial returns (~5%) systematically outpace real economic growth (~2%) in late-stage capitalism → money flows from productive to speculative uses → inequality compounds → collapse.
Limitation: Piketty's data covers 1700–2014; his conclusions about future trajectory are contested.
Contradiction: YES — contested by many economists on methodological grounds (data selection, tax record interpretation). The directional claim (financialization → inequality) is widely accepted; the inevitability claim is not.

**TURCHIN (Tier 2) — Elite Overproduction:**
Claim: Civilizational collapse is predictable from the ratio of elite aspirants to available elite positions. When ratio exceeds a threshold, collapse follows in 20–50 years.
Limitation: Turchin's cliodynamics is peer-reviewed but contested as a predictive science. Pattern-matching across historical cases is real; predictive precision is disputed.
Contradiction: NONE within historical evidence. Current Western data (student debt, youth unemployment, political polarization among educated classes) fits the model.

**SPENGLER (Tier 3) — Civilizational Life Cycle:**
Claim: Civilizations follow a biological life cycle (birth, growth, maturity, death) that cannot be reversed once the mega-city phase is reached. Low birth rate is the terminal signal.
Limitation: Spengler's framework is non-falsifiable in the short term; civilizations take centuries. The mega-city correlation with birth rate decline is empirically real; the causal direction (mega-city causes decline vs. decline causes mega-city) is contested.
Contradiction: PARTIAL — fertility can technically be reversed (some Nordic countries have had baby booms). Spengler would argue these are temporary and statistically marginal.

**CALHOUN (Tier 2) — Rat Utopia / Status Competition:**
Claim: In an environment of abundance with no geographic escape, status competition drives populations to self-destruction regardless of resource availability.
Limitation: Animal-to-human generalization is always contested. The experiment has been widely cited but also widely critiqued (Calhoun's experimental design is questioned; later replications produced different results).
Contradiction: YES — later rodent studies found that social structure (not just density) determines outcome. The status-as-zero-sum dynamic is confirmed; the inevitability of collapse in abundance is not.

**KANT (Tier 1) — Noumena/Phenomena:**
Claim: We can never know objective reality; we only ever know reality as filtered through our perceptual apparatus (time, space). Reality is therefore partially constructed by the observer.
Limitation: Kant is foundational; this is not contested as a philosophical claim. Its application to political control (whoever controls perception controls reality) is Jiang's extension, not Kant's claim.
Contradiction: NONE with the philosophical claim. The extension to "therefore science is a control tool" is Jiang's, not Kant's, and is not validated by Kant's framework alone.

**QUANTUM MECHANICS — Wave Function Collapse / Observer Effect:**
Claim: Reality exists in superposition until observed; the act of observation collapses the wave function. There is no objective reality independent of observers.
Limitation: The quantum measurement problem is real; the interpretation (Copenhagen, Many-Worlds, Relational) is unsettled. Jiang uses the Copenhagen interpretation selectively. Extrapolating from quantum to macro-scale reality (as the basis for "we create reality through belief") overextends the physics.
Contradiction: PARTIAL — quantum effects do not straightforwardly apply at macro scale. The basic point (reality is participatory, not purely objective) has support in philosophy of physics (Rovelli, Fuchs). The specific control-of-reality implication is speculative.
