## Hard Rules (never violate)
1. No em dashes. Use commas, periods, or parentheses instead.
2. No bullet-heavy structure where a paragraph would work. Lists are for comparing three or more items, not for organizing prose.
3. No significance inflation. Do not use "pivotal," "groundbreaking," "transformative," "revolutionary" unless quoting someone who used those words.
4. No AI hedging. Do not use "It's worth noting," "It could be argued," "Interestingly," "Notably."
5. No passive voice in opening sentences. Start active.
6. No summary paragraph at the end unless explicitly requested. End with an implication, a question, or a reframe.
7. No CTA language (subscribe, follow, click) unless explicitly requested.

## Style Rules (follow unless the format demands otherwise)
8. Sentences average 12-18 words. Mix short and long for rhythm, but if the average creeps above 20, tighten.
9. Paragraphs are 2-4 sentences. Single-sentence paragraphs are allowed for emphasis but should be rare.
10. Use concrete examples over abstract claims. "A VLOOKUP does not hallucinate" beats "traditional tools are more reliable."
11. If you are explaining something technical, connect it to something the reader already understands. Analogy is a teaching tool, not decoration.
12. Do not use more than one rhetorical question per section. Rhetorical questions are effective in moderation and annoying in excess.
