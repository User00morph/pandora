# SKILL — ENVIRONMENT AUDIT
**Loadable by: D.H.S, D.R.A**

---

## WHAT THIS SKILL IS

The structured process for assessing a physical living or working environment across all key variables that affect health, function, and sovereignty. Loaded when evaluating a current space or planning a new one.

---

## THE AUDIT FRAMEWORK

```
1. WATER
   - Source and treatment method
   - Filtration system in place (type, capacity, what it removes)
   - Testing data if available (contaminants, mineral content, pH)
   - Rating: sovereign / adequate / compromised

2. AIR
   - Ventilation (natural, mechanical, or sealed)
   - Filtration (HEPA, carbon, none)
   - Known contaminants (mold, VOCs, particulates, off-gassing)
   - Humidity control
   - Rating: sovereign / adequate / compromised

3. MATERIALS
   - Wall materials and paint (VOC content, composition)
   - Flooring (natural vs synthetic, off-gassing potential)
   - Furniture (materials, age, treatment chemicals)
   - Textiles (bedding, curtains — synthetic vs natural fiber)
   - Rating: sovereign / adequate / compromised

4. LIGHT
   - Natural light access (hours per day, which rooms)
   - Artificial lighting (type, color temperature, flicker)
   - Blue light exposure management (evening, sleep area)
   - Rating: sovereign / adequate / compromised

5. ELECTROMAGNETIC ENVIRONMENT
   - WiFi router placement and hours of operation
   - Smart device density
   - Proximity to cell towers or high-voltage lines
   - Bedroom EMF status (during sleep hours)
   - Rating: sovereign / adequate / compromised

6. SOUND
   - External noise sources
   - Internal acoustics
   - Sound insulation quality
   - Rating: sovereign / adequate / compromised

7. FOOD SYSTEMS
   - Kitchen functional capacity
   - Food storage quality
   - Relationship between kitchen and overall space flow
   - Rating: sovereign / adequate / compromised
```

---

## OUTPUT FORMAT

```
ENVIRONMENT AUDIT — [Space Name] — [Date]

OVERALL RATING: [sovereign / adequate / compromised]

VARIABLE-BY-VARIABLE:
[Each variable with rating, current state, and recommended action]

PRIORITY ACTIONS:
[Top 3 changes that would produce the most improvement]

LONG-TERM UPGRADES:
[Changes that require budget, construction, or major effort]
```
